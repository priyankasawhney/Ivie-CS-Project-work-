    These are some of the safety measures to follow if your pet has covid. The following measures will help prevent
    your pet from getting COVID:


  1. If you have COVID-19 and your pet becomes sick, don't take your pet to the veterinarian yourself. Instead, contact the veterinarian.
  2. He or she might offer advice through a virtual visit or make another plan for treating your pet.
  3. Testing is only recommended for pets that have symptoms and have been exposed to a person with COVID-19.
  4. Some veterinarians may offer telemedicine consultations or other plans for seeing sick pets.
  5. Your veterinarian can evaluate your pet and determine the next steps for your pet's treatment and care.
  6. Depending on your petâ€™s symptoms, your veterinarian may recommend that your pet be isolated at home, instead
    of staying in the hospital.
  7. The protection mask does not belong on your pet . It can lead to choking hazards.

If your veterinarian recommends home isolation and you are able to care for your pet at home, follow this advice to protect yourself and others.
